I'm Kyle Kjorsvik, a senior DevOps engineer with over 20 years in software — originally as a
developer, now focused on platform engineering. That path matters: I build infrastructure the
way a developer would, with the same attention to interfaces, ergonomics, and not making other
people's lives harder.

I care about boring, reliable systems. Good developer experience. Automating the toil out of
operations so product teams can ship without thinking about the plumbing.

## What I work on

Platform engineering — internal tooling, golden paths, and self-service infrastructure. CI/CD
pipelines that are fast and trustworthy. Cloud and Kubernetes architectures sized to the actual
problem, not the resume. Observability that makes systems legible before they break.

## How I work

I learn by building. Not tutorials, not sandboxes — actual projects, end to end, in production
or close to it. The projects on this site reflect that: each one is a deliberate vehicle for
going deep on something real. I prefer simple, well-understood tools over novelty, and I'd
rather delete code than add it.

I also run a homelab that's gotten out of hand in the best way — Proxmox, self-hosted Git, my
own CI runners, a kubeadm cluster, and more services than I'd like to admit. It's where I break
things on purpose before I break them at work.
